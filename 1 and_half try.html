Overview

Peptide alkaloids are a class of natural compounds found in plants. They are composed of both peptides and alkaloids and have a wide range of biological activity, such as medicinal, toxic, and defensive properties. These compounds are of great interest in the field of biochemistry and medicine because of their unique chemical structures and potential therapeutic applications.

Examples of Peptide Alkaloids

  • Coniine (found in poison hemlock)
  • d-tubocurarine (found in curare)
  • Vincristine and vinblastine (used in cancer treatment)
  • Cyclo(His-Pro) and dermorphin (used as analgesics)

Uses

Peptide alkaloids have a wide range of uses, including medicinal, toxic, and defensive properties. They have been used in traditional medicine for centuries and have also been studied for their potential as drug leads. Some peptide alkaloids are used as analgesics, while others are used in cancer treatment.
